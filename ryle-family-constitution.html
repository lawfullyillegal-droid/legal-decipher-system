Constitution of the Most Worshipful Grand Council of Family Members in Arizona

Kingman, Mohave County, Arizona – 2026
Published by authority of the Grand Council

CONSTITUTION

ARTICLE I – Title and Seal of Grand Council

Section 1. This Grand Council shall be entitled "The Most Worshipful Grand Council of Family Members of The Ryle Family Nation in Arizona."

Section 2. It shall have a seal… affixed to all instruments issued by or under its authority.

ARTICLE II – Of Whom Composed

Section 8. The Grand Council shall be composed of:

  • Grand Leader (Most Worshipful)
  • Deputy Grand Leader
  • Senior & Junior Grand Overseers (Right Worshipful)
  • Grand Treasurer
  • Grand Secretary
  • Grand Advisor
  • Judge Advocate
  • Grand Chaplain
  • All Past Grand Officers
  • Leaders of chartered Family Councils

Section 9. Each officer and member must be a member of some Family Council within its jurisdiction.

ARTICLE III – Powers of Grand Council

Section 16. The Grand Council is the Supreme Power and Authority over all matters pertaining to the governance and welfare of the Family Nation, limited only by strict adherence to the Ancient Principles of the Nation.

Section 17 – Legislative Powers. The Grand Council shall possess full legislative authority to enact by-laws, rules, and regulations for the governance of all Family Councils and members within its jurisdiction, provided such enactments align with the fundamental principles and purposes of the Nation.

Section 18 – Executive Powers. The Grand Council, through its officers, shall execute and enforce all laws, regulations, and decisions made by the Council. The Grand Leader and Deputy Grand Leader shall oversee the implementation of all Council directives and ensure compliance throughout all subordinate councils.

Section 19 – Judicial Powers. The Grand Council shall maintain judicial authority to adjudicate disputes, interpret this Constitution and by-laws, and exercise disciplinary powers over members and officers. The Judge Advocate shall preside over judicial proceedings in accordance with established procedures and principles of justice.

ARTICLE IV – Communications & Treasury Provisions

Annual Communication

Section 25 – Treasury Operations.

  • Grand Treasurer Duties: The Grand Treasurer shall receive all moneys, keep accurate accounts in proper books, and pay out funds only by order of the Grand Council or as authorized by this Constitution.
  • Sources of Funds: Treasury funds shall be derived from:
    • Fees and assessments as established by the Grand Council
    • Uniform contributions from Family Councils
    • Proceeds from dissolved councils
    • Life memberships and endowments
    • Gifts and donations in accordance with the Nation's principles
  • Charity Committee: A committee composed of the Grand Leader and Grand Overseers is empowered to draw up to $2.00 (inflation-adjusted to current economic conditions) per case for the relief of distressed members, widows, orphans, and dependents of members in good standing.
  • Common Stock Fund: A common stock fund shall be maintained for handsome relief of members and their families facing extraordinary circumstances, medical emergencies, or financial hardship beyond the scope of regular charitable disbursements.
  • Investment and Protection: All funds shall be invested prudently with the primary objectives of capital preservation and steady growth, in accordance with the fiduciary duties of the officers and consistent with the asset protection purposes of the Nation.

Additional Treasury Provisions

  • All financial transactions must be documented and subject to annual audit by an independent committee appointed by the Grand Council.
  • No funds may be disbursed for purposes inconsistent with the charitable, educational, and mutual aid objectives of the Nation.
  • Emergency relief may be authorized by the Grand Leader in consultation with the Grand Treasurer for amounts exceeding normal charitable limits, subject to ratification by the Grand Council at its next meeting.
